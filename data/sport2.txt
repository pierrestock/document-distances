Kemba Walker scored a season-high 30 points on Friday night as the Charlotte Hornets improved their best start in 16 years to 4-1 by beating the Nets, 99-95, at Barclays Center.Nicolas Batum added 18 points and 9 rebounds, while Marvin Williams, who missed his first 10 shots, made a 3-pointer with 1 minute 5 seconds left after the Nets (2-4) had cut the lead to 1.The reserve Sean Kilpatrick scored 19 points for the Nets, who did not have their starting point guard, Jeremy Lin, available to face his former team because of a strained left hamstring that is expected to keep him sidelined for at least two weeks. Charlotte’s only other point was a free throw by Ramon Sessions. The Nets led by 13 in the first half and were still ahead by 55-43 early in the third quarter before Walker and Cody Zeller, who played just five minutes in the first half because of foul trouble, turned it around. A basket by Walker capped an 18-3 run that gave Charlotte a 61-58 lead, and it was still a 3-point game going to the fourth quarter.The score remained close across the final period, and the lead was down to 92-91 when Williams made a 3-pointer from the corner. Walker followed with a free throw to make it 96-91, and the Nets never got closer than 3 points again.
